“The Regional concept constitutes the core of geography.” –James (1929:195)

Throughout history regional geography has traditionally been largely descriptive in order to explain and show how regions are different and how a person’s geography determines development. Thinking about Darwin’s theories of evolution and natural selection it can be seen how important geography has been in the development humans physically and culturally.
